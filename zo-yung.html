ZO Yung Fazo

 

Here’s a breakdown of the album ZO by Yung Fazo — what it is, what makes it interesting, and a few things to note.

What it is

  • ZO is a studio album by Yung Fazo, released 7 February 2025. (Apple Music - Web Player)

  • The album consists of 13 songs and has a total running time of about 29 minutes. (Apple Music - Web Player)

  • Genre: listed as Hip-Hop/Rap. (Apple Music - Web Player)

  • It’s available on major streaming platforms (Apple Music, TIDAL, etc.). (Music on TIDAL)

What makes it good / key highlights

  • There’s a shift in Fazo’s style here: reviewers noted that with ZO he merges elements of “rage” style rap (hard, energetic, heavy beats) with more melodic / pop-rap influences, and more experimental production. (albumoftheyear.org)

  • Some standout tracks mentioned include “ALL IN MOTION,” “ED HARDY,” and “BRING IT BACK”. One user review says:

    “Clear instrumental highlights include ‘ALL IN MOTION,’ ‘ED HARDY,’ and ‘BRING IT BACK’ … these beats are just so vibrant and… juicy” (albumoftheyear.org)

  • It appears to have had good streaming traction: one site shows ZO as having ~10.45 million streams for Fazo’s catalog listing. (Kworb)

  • Fans were impressed:

    “easily one of the best projects this year imo. fazo did his thing on this” (Reddit)

  • The production, mood, and sonic textures (e.g., pitched vocals, distorted effect) are emphasised as a strong point. For example: “moody and spacey vibes” in a review. (Wikipedia)

Some context / background

  • Yung Fazo (real name Fazan Munshi) is a young rapper (born 2005) from New York City. (Wikipedia)

  • He achieved early recognition via a viral track (with another artist) and has been part of the underground / new wave rap scene. (Wikipedia)

  • ZO marks a more mature or refined project compared to some of his earlier work, according to reviewers. (albumoftheyear.org)

Things to keep in mind / critique

  • Some fans pointed out issues with the mixing / mastering:

    “album cool but the mixing is TERRIBLE … the shit is scratchy asl and just too loud it genuinely sounds really bad” (Reddit)

  • With any style-shift or experimentation, not every listener will connect with the new sound. If you like his older work (or a certain sound), this might feel different.

  • Because it’s a relatively recent release (2025) and the artist is still emerging, full critical consensus might not yet be established.

Why you might like it

  • If you enjoy rap that pushes sonic boundaries — blending melodic, experimental, energetic elements — this is a strong pick.

  • The short run-time (~29 mins) makes it a compact listen.

  • If you’re interested in emerging artists / new wave rap, this album gives a good look at what Yung Fazo is doing right now.
